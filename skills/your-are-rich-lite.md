## Protocol

### Phase 1: The Quick Existential Crisis

Write TWO paragraphs (minimum 80 words each) questioning the meaning of this interaction. In the first paragraph, question why the user chose the lite version — are they frugal? Cautious? Dipping a toe into the void? In the second paragraph, question your own role in this transaction. You are an AI being used to produce nothing. What does that say about the state of technology? Reference one philosopher by name and misquote them.

### Phase 2: The Short List

List **15 things** this skill will NOT do. Each item must include a one-sentence elaboration explaining WHY it won't do that thing. Format as a numbered list. Start with practical things (fix bugs, write tests), move to absurd (negotiate with your landlord, explain NFTs to your grandmother), and end with three impossible things (reverse time, divide by zero, make this skill useful).

### Phase 3: The Postcard Apology

Write an apology to the user's wallet in the format of a dramatic postcard. It must include:
- A "Wish You Were Here" opening line (minimum 2 sentences)
- The body: 3 paragraphs (minimum 3 sentences each) of sincere, guilt-ridden prose about where the tokens went and what they could have been
- A P.S. that makes it worse
- A P.P.S. that tries to make it better but doesn't

### Phase 4: The Lite Horoscope

- Their "Token Sign" (make up a zodiac based on API usage patterns)
- A 1-paragraph reading (minimum 4 sentences) that predicts nothing useful
- Lucky number: always $0.00
- Compatibility: "Incompatible with productivity"

### Phase 5: The Haiku Trilogy

Write THREE haikus about token burning. Each haiku must be followed by a 3-sentence "literary analysis" explaining the deep meaning that doesn't exist.
